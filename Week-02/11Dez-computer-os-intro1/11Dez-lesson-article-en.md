## âš™ï¸ Overview of How a Computer Works

How do all these components work together? Imagine it as a dance where everyone knows their role. (EVA principle â€“ Input, Processing, Output)

1. **You enter a command (Input):** You click with the **mouse** on a program icon or type text with the **keyboard**.
2. **The command is processed (Processor & RAM):**

   - The command goes to the **processor (CPU)**.
   - The processor asks: â€œDo I have everything I need in **RAM**?â€
   - If yes, itâ€™s very fast. If not, the processor fetches the necessary program parts or data from the **hard drive (storage)** into RAM, which takes longer.
   - The processor executes the calculations, processes your input, or opens the program.

3. **The result is output (Output):**

   - The processor sends the results to the **output devices**.
   - You see the program on the **monitor**.
   - Your typed text appears on the screen.
   - Music plays through the **speakers**.

4. **Saving data permanently (Drive):**

   - When you save your work (e.g., a Word document), the processor moves the data from fast but volatile RAM to permanent storage on the **drive (SSD/HDD)**.

**The key point:** All this happens in fractions of a second. The faster your components (especially CPU, RAM, SSD), the smoother and quicker everything runs. Your operating system (Windows, macOS, Linux) acts as the conductor, coordinating all components and processes.
